The reason why

Initially, I thought I would study forensic science and if I didn't get accepted I would take a gap year. During this gap year I will live in Salamanca for half a year, so that I would be able to speak Spanish fluently afterwards. The other half year would give me the chance to sign in for Forensic Science for a second time and hopefully get accepted this time and look more into other studies I might be interested in. There were a few other studies that I might enjoy as well, namely Industrial Design (industrieel ontwerpen) and Computer Science and Engineering. But I didn't sign up for these studies this year because the both of them have selections as well and in the Netherlands you are allowed to sign up for only two sttudies with a selection. Therefore, I would have to choose between Industrial Design and Computer Science and Engineering, while I didn't know enougn about those 2 and thus wouldn't be able to know which of the two I would enjoy the most.

Salamanca

In 2018, My family and I slept in Salamanca for a night and explored the sity a bit. I immediately loved it, there are a lot of beautiful old buildings and a huge square with a lot cute restaurants. The vibe is amazing, everywhere you look you see groups of students enjoying themselves, shops selling swords and other Middle Ages stuff and old Roman buildings. All those things make me really want to go and live there for half a year, learn to speak Spanish fluent and maybe follow a few courses. My aunt also lived in Salamanca for a while and she really liked it as well.

For me, it is quite important to be able to speak a lot of languages. My English is already pretty good, but my Spanish isn't as good as I want it to be. I have already had 7 years of Spanish classes and I still don't really speak it. That is the biggest reason I want to go to Salamanca, since the best way to learn a language is to just speak it and practice it.

Corona Virus

The Corona virus causes a problem. Because of it, it isn't as easy to take a gap year as it would normally be. At this moment, Spain is a dangerous country to go to. Spaniards barely go outside themselves, that is how cautious they are. So, there isn't any certainty that it will be safe in September to go and live there. And, if I am not able to do this in my gap year, taking a gap year wouldn't be as usefull and productive as I want it to be. If this will be the case, I don't want to take a gap year. But that does mean I have to figure out what study I would want to do if I don't get accepted to Forensic Science and that can't be one of the other studies I was maybe interested in since those are deadlines have passed. I now have to find a completely new study that I would enjoy and doesn't have a selection so I can actually do that next year.

My first choice will still be Forensic Science, but I will now have enough time to look more into the other studies containing selections. Or find a study that doesn't have selections, but I do find very interesting!
